1. box-out, Stand-up Looped
  • Were they in the proper posture on bottom (toes not curled, pointed out)?
  • Did they box out, with their wrist outside their elbow (so that the opponent cannot reach a cross wrist)?
  • Did they pivot up to their feet (never taking an actual step up?)
  • Was there a knee-slide or two inbetween the first and second pivots?
  • Once in the rear standing position, did they maintain a boxout position, with their elbow touching their own thigh? Were they in a wrestling stance? Did he get finger control?
    		•
    2. Switch Looped
  • Were they in the proper posture on bottom (toes not curled, pointed out)?
  • Did they cross-over their hands and step up at the same time?
  • Did they land on their hip (not flat on their butt)?
  • Did they finish by attacking a high crotch?
    		•
    3. Bronco Looped
  • Were they in the proper posture on bottom (toes not curled, pointed out)?
  • Did they throw their butt up into the air, and then get really low?
    		•
    4. Knee-bounce standup Looped
  • Did they start in a pike position, and then put one knee down while bringing their trail leg in?
  • Did they bounce the correct knee (whichever side the opponent is on)
  • Did they spring up off their fingertips?
  • Did the knee that bounced end up being the foot in front (kids incorrectly do a drop-step)
  • Did they finish in a box-out position
    		•
    5. Sit-out turn-in basics (without a partner) Looped
  • Did they step up as they crossed their hands over?
  • Were they in the proper posture on bottom (toes not curled, pointed out)?
  • Did they land on their butt and immediately tuck their arms tight, with their head up and shoulders shrugged?
  • Did they land on their forehead (eyebrows) (not the side of their head)?
  • Did they take a big step to a tripod position, then circle, and then drop to their hip?
  • When they dropped to their hip, did they form a wall with their elbow and knee?
    		•
    6. Sit-out turn-in (out the back door)(with a partner) Looped
  • Same bullet points from the sit-out turn-in without a partner
    		•
    7. Sit-out turn-in, Peterson Looped
  • Same bullet points from the sit-out turn-in without a partner
  • Did he circle once he reached the tripod?
  • Did he form a wall when hitting the Peterson?
  • This movement should only be hit after attempting to come out the back door
    		•
    8. Fat-man roll Looped
  • Did he grab the wrist and box-out?
  • Did he keep the wall formed?
  • Did he go to his forehead? (this is a common mistake, they should go to their butt)
    		•
    9. defending the suck-back Looped
  • Did the wrestlers post his hands and scoot his butt back further than his hands?
    		•
    10. Fatman roll from the sit-out Looped
  • Did they turn towards the opponent’s head?
  • Did they post a forearm and hand?
  • Did they bring their knee to their elbow forming a wall?
  • Did they use a handcuff?
    		•
    11. Gizoni Looped
  • Did they lift their arm up and turn away?
    		•
    12. Head-catch Gizoni Looped
  • Did they turn away from the head?
  • Did they post their free elbow?
  • Did they finish with a half nelson / cement mixer?
    		•
    13. Half nelson with short-hook finish Looped
  • Did they use the mat sandwich to get their opponents head down?
  • Did they start with a half that was deep around the neck (fingers approaching the throat)?
  • Did they start with good pressure, cheek to cheek, butt high in the air (partner should not be able to lift his head off the mat)?
  • Did they pull the opponent’s wrist out before scooping the short hook?
  • Was their short hook above the opponent’s elbow?
  • Did they finish with their knee tight to the opponent’s chest?
